# Brivia Eats — Menu Naming System
## A Framework for Translating Food Without Erasing Culture

---

## 1. Purpose of the Naming System

The Brivia Naming System exists to solve a recurring problem in international travel:

**How to present unfamiliar local food in a way that reduces anxiety and uncertainty, while preserving cultural identity and meaning.**

Literal translation fails because food names are often:
- symbolic rather than descriptive
- rooted in history or metaphor
- disconnected from ingredients
- culturally encoded

At the same time, complete foreignization creates fear, hesitation, and ordering mistakes.

Brivia’s system is designed to deliberately manage this tension.

---

## 2. Core Design Principles

### 2.1 Clarity Before Curiosity

In travel contexts, users prioritize:
- safety
- predictability
- dietary certainty
- social confidence

**Every dish must clearly communicate what the food actually is before inviting cultural exploration.**

---

### 2.2 Cultural Identity Must Be Preserved

Food names carry accumulated cultural meaning.  
Erasing names in favor of ingredient lists prevents dishes from becoming recognizable cultural symbols.

Brivia preserves original dish names to enable:
- learning through repetition
- global recognition over time
- continuity across markets and languages

---

### 2.3 Progressive Disclosure

Not all users want the same depth.

Information is revealed in layers:
- immediate clarity
- optional cultural depth
- expandable storytelling

This avoids overwhelming users while rewarding curiosity.

---

## 3. The Three-Layer Naming Structure

Every dish in Brivia **must** follow the same three-layer structure.

---

### 3.1 Primary Name — Cultural Anchor

**Definition**  
The Primary Name is the original dish name, presented in full romanization with spaced syllables, with native script available.

**Rules**
- Fully romanized (no mixed-language names)
- Spaced by syllable (pinyin-style)
- Language-pure
- Stable across markets

**Purpose**
- Serves as the canonical identifier of the dish
- Preserves cultural authenticity
- Enables global recognition and memory formation

**Examples**
- Xi Hu Xun Yu (西湖熏鱼)
- Dong Po Rou (东坡肉)
- Long Jing Xia Ren (龙井虾仁)

**Rationale**  
Romanized names are pronounceable, repeatable, and learnable.  
They allow users to talk about dishes without flattening them into generic descriptions.

---

### 3.2 Secondary Name — Ordering Clarity

**Definition**  
The Secondary Name is a plain-language description of the dish’s core components.

**Must include**
- main ingredients
- cooking method
- primary flavor profile (when relevant)

**Rules**
- No metaphor
- No cultural assumptions
- Honest and expectation-setting
- Written for non-experts

**Purpose**
- Reduces ordering anxiety
- Prevents unpleasant surprises
- Supports dietary decision-making

**Examples**
- Crispy Sweet-and-Sour Mandarin Fish, Scored & Deep-Fried
- Slow-Braised Pork Belly in Soy Sauce, Rice Wine & Sugar

**Rationale**  
Clarity belongs here — **never** in the Primary Name.

---

### 3.3 One-Line Story — Cultural & Sensory Bridge

**Definition**  
A short, optional sentence connecting the dish’s sensory experience with its cultural meaning.

**May include**
- taste, aroma, texture, or presentation
- naming origin, tradition, or local significance

**Rules**
- One sentence only
- No fictionalization
- Optional and expandable
- Informative, not romanticized

**Purpose**
- Adds depth without overload
- Makes the dish memorable
- Transforms food into experience

**Examples**
- Named for its squirrel-like shape after the fish is scored and fanned before frying.
- Associated with poet Su Dongpo, this dish is known for its rich flavor and tender texture.

---

## 4. Romanization & Spacing Rules

**Default Rule**
- Use **spaced syllables** for all Primary Names.

**Why**
- Improves readability and pronunciation confidence
- Aligns with how non-native readers parse unfamiliar words
- Avoids arbitrary compounding decisions

**Progression**
As dishes become familiar, names may naturally collapse over time.  
Brivia always stores the spaced form as the canonical version.

---

## 5. Why Partial Translation Is Avoided

Mixed-language Primary Names (e.g. *Xi Hu Smoked Fish*) are avoided because they:
- break linguistic consistency
- reduce memorability
- weaken cultural symbolism
- create scaling problems across languages

Ingredient clarity is handled **exclusively** in the Secondary Name.

Legacy global terms (e.g. *Kung Pao Chicken*) are treated as exceptions, not templates.

---

## 6. Universality Across Markets

The structure remains constant across cuisines and directions of travel.

What changes is **emphasis, not format**:
- In unfamiliar contexts, clarity dominates
- In familiar contexts, stories recede
- Over time, Primary Names may stand alone

This allows Brivia to scale globally without redesigning the system.

---

## 7. Role of AI & Human Verification

**AI assists by**
- generating structured drafts
- identifying common dish patterns
- proposing names and explanations

**Humans verify**
- ingredient accuracy
- cooking methods
- dietary and allergy risks
- cultural correctness

Verified outputs become reusable ground truth.

Accuracy and trust take priority over speed.

---

## 8. What the System Ultimately Enables

- Confident ordering for travelers
- Preservation of local food identity
- Accumulation of global cultural recognition
- A shared vocabulary for food across borders

**Brivia does not simplify food.  
It makes it understandable — without taking its name away.**
